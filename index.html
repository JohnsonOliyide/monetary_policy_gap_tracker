Monetary Policy Gap Tracker

Tracking whether monetary policy is restrictive or accommodative across alternative r* estimates.

This dashboard compares the real policy rate with alternative estimates of the natural rate of interest, r*, to assess whether monetary policy appears restrictive, neutral, or accommodative under the selected policy rate and inflation-expectations measure.

Policy gap = (Policy rate − Expected inflation) − r*

Positive policy gaps indicate restrictive policy. Negative policy gaps indicate accommodative policy. Values close to zero suggest a neutral or ambiguous stance.

    Methodology

    The dashboard calculates the monetary policy gap as:

    Policy gapj,t = (it − πet) − r*j,t

    Here, it is the selected policy rate, πet is the selected expected-inflation measure, and r*j,t is the natural-rate estimate from measure j. A positive gap means the real policy rate is above the selected r* estimate, suggesting a restrictive stance. A negative gap means the real policy rate is below r*, suggesting an accommodative stance.

    Moving-average inflation-expectation proxies

    The moving-average options are not survey responses. They are adaptive proxies for expected inflation, constructed from recent inflation. This is useful because the approach is transparent, reproducible, available over long samples, and close to the way several r* models construct the ex-ante real interest rate.

    For monthly policy-gap calculations, the proxy for price index k is the average of the previous 12 annualized month-over-month inflation rates:

    πe,kt = (1 / 12) × Σj=112 πkt−j, MoM annualized

    The current month is excluded, so the expectation proxy for month t only uses inflation data through month t−1. The same method is applied separately to core PCE, headline PCE, core CPI, and headline CPI.

    For quarterly policy-gap calculations, the proxy is the average of the previous four annualized quarter-over-quarter inflation rates:

    πe,kq = (1 / 4) × Σj=14 πkq−j, QoQ annualized

    The dashboard uses the quarterly construction for quarterly r* measures rather than interpolating quarterly r* estimates into monthly values.

    Survey and model-based expectations

    The survey/model-based options use published expected-inflation measures rather than moving averages of realized inflation. The Cleveland Fed option is a model-based one-year expected inflation series. Michigan and the New York Fed Survey of Consumer Expectations are household-based one-year expectations. The Survey of Professional Forecasters is a professional-forecaster expectation and is quarterly. When SPF is selected, all displayed policy-gap calculations switch to quarterly frequency.

    Frequency treatment: monthly r* measures remain monthly, while quarterly r* measures remain quarterly. The dashboard does not interpolate quarterly r* estimates into monthly observations. When a quarterly inflation-expectations measure such as SPF is selected, all policy-gap calculations are reported at quarterly frequency.

    Sources

    The dashboard is designed to use publicly available series from Federal Reserve sources, including the Kansas City Fed, New York Fed, Richmond Fed, Cleveland Fed, FRED and FOMC Summary of Economic Projections releases, together with author calculations.

    The current prototype uses illustrative sample values. The included update pipeline is a starting point for replacing the sample data with live or periodically refreshed public data.

    About

    The Monetary Policy Gap Tracker is a research dashboard designed to compare assessments of the stance of monetary policy across alternative estimates of the natural rate of interest. It does not estimate a new r* series and does not combine existing estimates into a single index. Instead, it shows how the implied policy stance changes across existing model-based, market-based and policymaker-based measures.
